How I missed the moon ... and the schedule

Contents

It’s less than a week since I’ve set up the blog and I already missed the first deadline.

To be honest…

This does segue into a fun topic to explore, the idea of expectations. A generally good way to live life is to under-promise and over-deliver. Assuming a bit of common sense about not bottoming out on expectations, you will always surprise yourself and other people (Great success!). I have generally done the opposite, setting unrealistic expectations and ending with an average to above-average performance.

This blog is currently fairly average by my performance standards: Use Hugo, write, push to github on time.

I apologize for not getting this post out on time. I hope Monday’s post will arrive on time but I’m moving into residence that day so anything goes :)

A lesser known side effect of not meeting expectations

… is that one gets a lot of practice with completing “post-mortems”. I’ll share the one I wrote up in response to being late for the first serious blog post:

Why it happened

  • I’m lazy
  • Computers are hard
  • Nuka World just released for Fallout 4

How I can remedy the situation

  • Post as soon as possible
  • Apologize profusely
  • Add extra markdown flair to distract from late-ness (Say hello to pictures and a table of contents!)

How I’ll prevent it from happening again

  • Write a quick outline of the next post immediately after writing current post
  • Make a habit of practicing periodic concentration to ward off procrastination
  • Edit the blog timestamp Post something on time and edit later if time permits

To conclude

Enough one-liners to last you a week are (probably) coming out on Monday - I’ve already written up the outline. That’s all folks!
